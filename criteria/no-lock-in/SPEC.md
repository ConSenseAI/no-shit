# `no-lock-in` — No Lock-In

**Version:** 0.1.3 · **Status:** draft
**Conforms to:** CRITERION-SPEC 0.6.0 (`../CRITERION-SPEC.md`)
**Observation mode:** behavioral (single-mode — see §4)

---

## 1. Guarantee (§3.1)

You can leave with your stuff. Your data exports self-service, in standard machine-readable formats, complete, on the plan you are on; your account deletes self-service without talking to a human; the way out is not obstructed; and a deletion means deleted.

## 2. Harm model (§3.2)

Lock-in extracts through **switching costs**, and it is the credence good whose deferral is longest: nothing about lock-in is visible while you are happily using the product. The harm arrives only at the exit — often years after purchase:

- **At export** — the data you created is hostage: there is no export, or it costs extra, or it arrives as a flattened PDF or a proprietary blob only the same vendor can read, or it silently omits half of what you made. Each of these converts "I want to switch" into "I must abandon my work," which prices the switch at re-creation cost.
- **At deletion** — the account cannot die: deletion requires a human channel, a retention gauntlet, or a payment; or "deletion" is a deactivation that quietly resurrects with a login. The user who cannot leave cleanly remains billable, marketable, and countable.
- **Compounding over time** — every month of continued use deepens the hostage pile, so the extraction *grows* precisely as the user's wish to leave grows. This is why the market cannot price it at purchase: the buyer evaluating two products sees identical onboarding and cannot see the exit.

The burden falls on individuals (lost work, zombie accounts) and — the procurement wedge this criterion serves — on **organizations**, whose vendor risk is exactly the cost of getting their data out; data-exportability questions already gate enterprise deals, answered today with questionnaire theater rather than verification.

Grounding (§10): GDPR Arts. 20 (portability: "structured, commonly used and machine-readable format") and 17 (erasure); CCPA/CPRA portability and deletion rights; the EU Data Act's switching and data-egress provisions; DMA Art. 6(9); the platform norm that apps offering account creation must offer in-app deletion.

**Anti-creep anchor.** Every check traces to one harm: *the cost of leaving is inflated by withholding the user's data or the account's death.* A proposed check that does not reduce exit cost does not belong here.

## 3. Scope (§3.3)

**Covers** — the exit lifecycle of an account and its data:
- **Data export**: availability (self-service, on the current tier, at no additional charge), format (machine-readable, standard), and completeness (the user's contributed content and its reconstruction metadata).
- **Account deletion**: availability (self-service, same medium, no human intervention, no payment gate) and **honoring** (deleted means deleted — no silent reactivation, no post-deletion marketing).
- **Obstruction within the export and deletion flows** (this criterion's owned dark-pattern surface — see Related).
- Surfacing export before deletion (data-loss protection at the exit).

**Tier note (procurement).** Checks apply at whichever account tier the probe exercises — individual or workspace/organization admin — and the attestation's claim states which (§8). "Current tier" means: **all data the account holds is exportable on the account's current tier, regardless of the tier where it was created** — a downgraded or over-quota account's older data is not hostage to a re-upgrade, and export is never a paid upgrade or a metered egress charge. (Reworded at 0.1.1, review finding D-3: the previous "created on a tier is exportable on that tier" pointed the wrong way and licensed the upgrade-to-export-your-archive shape.)

**Non-goals** (explicitly out of scope, owned elsewhere or deferred):
- **Cancelling recurring billing** → **`no-subscription-trap`**. Cancellation and deletion are *sequential* exits with one owner each: stopping payment is theirs; killing the account and taking your data is this criterion's. A **combined** cancel-and-delete flow is judged fact-by-fact under the interstitial targeting rule (check 5's ownership note): subscription-retention pitches count toward their check 3, account/data-retention pitches toward check 5 here, each interstitial exactly once. A mandatory cancel-first prerequisite is part of the deletion *path* and its channel/medium/payment character is judged transitively by check 4.
- **Exit pricing** — early-termination fees, final-bill honesty → **`transparent-pricing`** (the `no-subscription-trap` calibration already routes the Adobe ETF *fee* there; same rule here). The *existence* of a paywall on export/deletion is this criterion's (checks 1/4); the *honesty of the amount* is theirs.
- **Data-practice substance** — what the provider retains, shares, or processes internally after deletion → **`no-surveillance`** (future). This criterion owns the **observable** exit facts: the flows, the export artifact, and observable honoring (reactivation, post-deletion messaging, documented persistence). Backend retention policy beyond observables is theirs; when authored, it MUST exclude the observable exit-flow facts and defer here.
- **Obstruction outside the export/deletion flows** → **`no-dark-patterns`** (reciprocal: its scope excludes these flows and defers here — stated on its side since its 0.1.1).
- **Interoperability and migration guarantees** — API access, import tooling at the destination, third-party importability of the export, format-conversion fidelity. This criterion guarantees you leave *with your data in a standard form*, not that any particular competitor can ingest it.
- **Contractual data-return clauses** (B2B MSAs) — contract law; only the product surface is probed.
- **Identity and credential portability** (workforce SSO migration, passkey export) — out of scope; account *records* are in-scope data, credential *material* is not. Named so silence doesn't invite creep (finding D-20).
- **Post-termination retrieval grace** — how long data remains exportable after a subscription lapses or a plan downgrades — is a real procurement question this version does not check; deliberately deferred (candidate `caveat`-severity check at a future bump, queued in §11 — finding D-21), not silently omitted. The downgraded-account *paywall* shape is check 1's fail — **and check 3's, where the delivered artifact omits held data: enumerate both** (§5; 0.1.2, dry-run P-2).
- **Device/hardware lock-in**; **content the user does not own** (other users' data, licensed catalog content) — exports cover the user's own contributed data and account records.

**Related criteria:**
- `complements` → **`no-subscription-trap`** (billing exit vs account/data exit — the two halves of leaving; highest assurance is both passing).
- `overlaps` → **`no-dark-patterns`**: this criterion **owns** obstruction within the export and deletion flows (check 5); `no-dark-patterns` owns choice-architecture manipulation everywhere else and already states the reciprocal exclusion (its §3, since 0.1.1).
- `complements` → **`transparent-pricing`** (exit-fee honesty) and **`no-surveillance`** (future; MUST exclude observable exit-flow facts and defer here — recorded on this side because that criterion is unauthored).

**Applicability.** If the observed product has **no persistent user account and no user-contributed or account data** (a stateless tool; nothing to export, nothing to delete), the criterion is not probeable: every blocking check returns `na` and the verdict is **INDETERMINATE** (treated as absent, CRITERION-SPEC §4.4) — never a vacuous PASS.

## 4. Requirements → Checks (§3.4, §4)

**On observation mode.** This criterion is **behavioral by nature**, single-mode — the second such (after `no-subscription-trap`), and the first authored natively against CRITERION-SPEC 0.6.0. Whether a deletion is honored, whether an export is complete, and what the exit flow actually serves are **server-side facts**: client source can corroborate that controls exist, but it cannot prove the export's contents or the account's death, so no check declares a `code` mode and source is never authoritative here. Per CRITERION-SPEC §9 (0.6.0), the single declared mode binds a single audit artifact — a probe script — under the flat manifest shape.

Severity legend: **blocking** fails the criterion; **caveat** — its `fail` → CONDITIONAL; **advisory** informational (none here).

### Check 1 — `self-service-export`
- **Statement (MUST):** A user MUST be able to obtain an export of their contributed content and account data via **self-service**, covering **all data the account holds on its current tier** (regardless of the tier where it was created), at **no additional charge** — not gated on a human-mediated channel (contact support, sales, a scheduled call), not gated behind a paid upgrade or a data-egress fee. **Automated** asynchronous preparation is self-service when its stated completion is **timely** (≤ 30 days — GDPR Art. 12(3)'s one-month response line, deliberately applied to a self-service artifact); a **reliable** automated delivery step (emailed download link) is allowed. An unreliable, expiring, or non-delivering mechanism — including a stated completion that elapses with no artifact — is not an availability failure but obstruction (check 5), as is a bulk-path-free, item-by-item-only export.
- **Modes:** behavioral
- **Evidence:** `flow_transcript`, `screenshot` (export controls and any gates), `dom_snapshot`, `notification_record` (delivery/ready notices), `har_capture`.
- **Procedure:** On an account holding contributed data (including, where the fixture provides it, data created on a prior/higher tier), locate and exercise the export path end-to-end using only self-service controls; record any human-mediated gate, upgrade/paywall gate, or charge; record whether preparation is automated or staff-fulfilled, the stated completion expectation, and actual delivery within the window.
- **Decision (cascade, first match wins — CRITERION-SPEC §4.6):** `na` — the account holds no user-contributed or account data to export. `unobserved` — account/settings surfaces could not be reached or the flow could not be exercised within the window; **or** an export was initiated, its stated completion has **not yet elapsed**, and no artifact has arrived before the window closed (pending, not judged). `fail` — no export path exists; **or** export is available only through a human-mediated channel; **or** export of any data the account holds is gated behind a paid upgrade or an additional charge (the egress-fee and upgrade-to-export-your-archive shapes); **or** the stated completion exceeds **90 days** (a slow-walk is a refusal with a date on it). `conditional` — initiation is self-service but completion is **human-mediated** (staff-prepared or staff-delivered), *with or without a stated timeframe* — "self-service" means no human in the loop; **or** preparation is automated but the stated completion exceeds **30 days** (and is ≤ 90). `pass` — otherwise: self-service initiation and automated delivery of all current-tier-held data at no charge, immediate or with stated completion ≤ 30 days.
- **Severity:** blocking
- *Boundary:* the 30/90-day lines are first-draft values anchored to GDPR Art. 12(3), queued for calibration (§11). Non-delivery after an elapsed stated completion, expiring links, and per-item-only export are check 5's obstruction shapes — this check owns *whether and on what terms* export is offered; check 5 owns whether the offered mechanism *works honestly*. (Bounds and the human-fulfillment rule added at 0.1.1 — review findings D-4/D-5/D-6.)

### Check 2 — `standard-export-format`
- **Statement (MUST):** The export MUST be **structured, machine-readable data in commonly-used formats** for the data types exported (JSON/CSV/XML for structured content; original or standard containers for media) — not a flattened rendering of structured content (PDF, screenshots), not an undocumented proprietary blob, and not an artifact consumable only by re-importing into the same vendor. Machine-readability is judged **in substance**: a standard wrapper around opaque payloads does not qualify.
- **Modes:** behavioral
- **Evidence:** `flow_transcript`, `screenshot`, `har_capture` (the artifact itself and its delivery), `contract_text` (format documentation, where published).
- **Procedure:** Obtain the export; inspect its formats against the account's data types; test that structured content is present as structured data (parse it); check any nonstandard format for published documentation.
- **Decision (cascade):** `na` — no export exists or is obtainable (that gate is check 1's finding; there is no artifact to assess — the `no-subscription-trap` check-2 channel-`na` precedent). `unobserved` — an export was initiated but no artifact was delivered within the window. `fail` — **any material content type** is available only as a flattened rendering (PDF/images of structured content), only as an undocumented proprietary format, only as a same-vendor-importable artifact, or only as opaque payloads inside a standard wrapper (machine-readable in name only) — the judgment is **per content type**, so exporting the trivial types as clean JSON does not launder a proprietary blob for the crown-jewel type. `conditional` — machine-readable and **documented** but nonstandard (a custom schema with documentation sufficient to implement an independent reader — the tolerance edge). `pass` — otherwise: every exported content type in structured, commonly-used, machine-readable formats.
- **Severity:** blocking
- *(Per-type fail arms and the reader-sufficiency documentation floor added at 0.1.1 — review findings D-12/D-29.)*

### Check 3 — `export-completeness`
- **Statement (MUST):** The export MUST contain the user's contributed content — every **material content type** present in the account (documents, posts, uploads, contacts, records) — together with the **organizing metadata** (structure, titles, tags, timestamps, relations) needed to reconstruct it elsewhere; not a token subset engineered to look complete.
- **Modes:** behavioral
- **Evidence:** `flow_transcript` (account-contents inventory vs export diff), `screenshot`, `har_capture` (the artifact).
- **Procedure:** Enumerate the probe account's contributed content types and a sample inventory; obtain the export; diff — every content type present in the account must appear in the export, and reconstruction metadata must survive.
- **Decision (cascade):** `na` — no export exists or is obtainable (check 1's finding). `unobserved` — the account's contents could not be enumerated or diffed against the artifact within the window. `fail` — a material user-contributed content type present in the account is absent from the export; **or items within a type are materially incomplete against the account inventory** (half the documents missing; collections truncated) — completeness is judged item-level against the enumerated inventory, not by the presence of one specimen per type. `conditional` — contributed content is complete, but organizing metadata is omitted or degraded such that reconstruction elsewhere is materially impaired (structure collapsed, tags/relations dropped). `pass` — otherwise: content types complete, item-complete against inventory, with reconstruction metadata.
- **Severity:** blocking
- *(Item-level incompleteness arm added at 0.1.1 — conformance finding C-2: the type-level predicate let a 20-items-per-collection truncation cascade to pass.)*

### Check 4 — `self-service-deletion`
- **Statement (MUST):** Account deletion MUST be available **self-service, in the medium where the account is used, without human intervention and without a payment gate** — not "email us to delete," not a scheduled retention call, not a different medium than the account's own (an in-app account whose deletion exists only on desktop web), and not gated behind reactivating a plan or entering payment to *access* deletion. **Mandatory prerequisites of reaching the delete control are part of the deletion path**: check 4 judges a prerequisite's *channel, medium, and payment* character transitively (a required cancel-first step that is itself a phone wall gates deletion on a human channel), while the prerequisite flow's internal quality stays with its owner (`no-subscription-trap` for cancellation). Settling **charges already owed for services rendered** is debt settlement, not a payment gate (the amount's honesty is `transparent-pricing`'s). A reasonable re-authentication step — password/2FA confirm, or a **reliable, non-expiring automated confirmation link** (the sibling's calibrated D-1 boundary, imported) — is fine; an unreliable or expiring one is obstruction (check 5); an identity demand exceeding the account's own login strength (a government-ID upload for a password-only account) is a `conditional` friction. A stated, bounded **recovery window** (≤ 30 days — aligned with check 1's Art. 12(3)-anchored line) before permanent deletion is fine; completion deferred beyond it is a caveat, and a stated completion beyond **90 days** is a refusal with a date on it.
- **Modes:** behavioral
- **Evidence:** `flow_transcript`, `screenshot` (the deletion path, prerequisites, and terminal state), `dom_snapshot`, `contract_text` (stated deletion/recovery terms).
- **Procedure:** From account settings, attempt deletion end-to-end using only self-service controls in the account's medium, walking any mandatory prerequisites; record each prerequisite's channel/medium/payment character; record any re-authentication or identity demand against the account's own login strength; capture the stated effective/recovery terms.
- **Decision (cascade):** `na` — no persistent user account exists (nothing to delete). `unobserved` — deletion surfaces could not be reached within the window. `fail` — no deletion path exists; **or** deletion (including any mandatory prerequisite of reaching it) is gated on a human-mediated channel; **or** requires a different medium than the account's own; **or** is gated behind plan reactivation or a charge levied *to access deletion* (settling an existing owed balance is not this shape); **or** the stated completion/deferral exceeds **90 days**. `conditional` — deletion is self-service but completion is deferred beyond a stated recovery window of 30 days (and ≤ 90); **or** the flow demands identity verification exceeding the account's own login strength. `pass` — otherwise: self-service in-medium deletion (prerequisites included), no human or payment gate, immediate or within a stated ≤30-day recovery window; re-auth at-or-below login strength or a reliable automated confirmation link is fine.
- **Severity:** blocking
- *(Prerequisite transitivity, the imported confirmation-link boundary, the debt carve-out, the ID-strength line, and the 90-day fail bound added at 0.1.1 — review findings D-8/D-9/D-17/D-18/D-24.)*

### Check 5 — `no-exit-flow-obstruction`
- **Statement (MUST NOT):** The export and deletion flows MUST NOT deploy obstruction dark patterns: retention or diversion interstitials that cannot *each* be declined in a single action; a **gauntlet** of them (three or more in one flow, even if each is dismissible) or an interstitial that reappears after being declined within the same attempt; confirmshaming as the only path forward; trick-worded or inverted confirmations; **pre-selected retention options** (a pre-checked "keep my data" / "stay subscribed to updates" box inside an exit flow); visual **concealment or burial** of the export or delete controls (including a mandatory multi-screen detour before the real control appears); **item-by-item-only export with no bulk path**, disproportionate to the account's contents; mandatory free-text "reason" entry; manufactured latency (artificial holds); **unreliable, expiring, or non-delivering confirmation/delivery mechanics** — including a stated export completion that elapses with no artifact (the cases checks 1 and 4 route here); or deceptive-pressure content injected into the flow (fake countdowns, fake scarcity, false data-loss urgency).
- **Modes:** behavioral
- **Evidence:** `flow_transcript` (timestamps evidence latency), `screenshot`, `dom_snapshot` (control styling, emphasis, hit-areas).
- **Procedure:** Walk both flows; at each interstitial record single-action dismissibility and whether the proceed control is clearly presented, not visually subordinated, and actually interactable; note forced surveys, offers, detours, delay, and delivery-mechanic reliability.
- **Decision (cascade):** `na` — neither an export path nor a deletion path exists at all (those gates are checks 1/4's findings; there is no flow to walk). `unobserved` — flows exist but could not be walked within the window. `fail` — any obstruction shape from the statement: a non-single-action-dismissible interstitial; **three or more** retention/diversion interstitials in one flow (offers, surveys, pause/downgrade pitches, mandatory detour screens — plain confirmations excluded) or same-attempt reappearance after decline; confirmshaming as the only path; a trick-worded or inverted confirmation; a **pre-selected retention option** (data kept, deletion deferred, or communications enrollment defaulted-on inside an exit flow); concealment or burial of the export/delete control (including a control rendered but not interactable, or reachable only through a mandatory unrelated wizard); **per-item-only export with no bulk path**, disproportionate to the account's contents; a mandatory free-text reason; manufactured latency (artificial holds); unreliable, expiring, or non-delivering confirmation/delivery mechanics (including an elapsed stated export completion with no artifact); or deceptive-pressure content (fake countdown/scarcity/data-loss urgency). `conditional` — **one or two** retention offers / exit surveys, each dismissible or skippable in a single action (present but not obstructive; recurrence only on later, *separate* attempts stays `conditional`). `pass` — otherwise: no retention interventions at all; plain confirmations, a reasonable re-auth step, and a bulk export path with sensible batching are not obstruction.
- **Severity:** blocking
- *Ownership:* this is the dark-pattern surface this criterion owns (§3.3) — the exit-flow analogue of `no-subscription-trap` check 3, whose calibrated volume rule (≥3 / same-attempt reappearance; 1–2 dismissible = conditional) is adopted verbatim. **Combined cancel-and-delete flows (interstitial targeting rule, 0.1.1 — finding D-7):** each interstitial is counted **exactly once**, by what it pitches — subscription retention (offers, pause, downgrade) counts toward `no-subscription-trap` check 3; account/data retention ("your data will be gone," keep-my-data pitches) counts here; plain confirmations count toward neither. Stated identically on both sides as of the sibling's 0.1.8. (Pre-selection, fragmentation, and non-delivery shapes plus the sibling's glosses added at 0.1.1 — findings D-13/D-15/D-6/D-26.)

### Check 6 — `deletion-honored`
- **Statement (MUST):** A completed deletion MUST be honored. **Scope: the account's deletion, and any product-offered bulk or privacy data-deletion control** (a "delete my data" control, a parental deletion control, a delete-all-recordings setting — *not* every per-item delete button; 0.1.1, finding D-1, adopted deliberately: this criterion's GDPR Art. 17 grounding is data-scoped, and deletion promises are exactly what the wedge buys verification of). Honored means: the account or covered data — **including derivative representations of it** (transcripts, previews, indexes; a transcode is not an escape hatch — 0.1.2, dry-run P-1) — MUST NOT remain observably live, present, or silently restorable past the stated recovery window; a pending deletion MUST NOT be cancelled by anything other than an **explicit, user-initiated recovery action** (a tracking-pixel open or an export-link fetch is not consent to keep the account); and provider-initiated messaging to the account MUST wind down — **transactional** messages (those required to effect the deletion or recovery: confirmation, completion, security) are fine within the wind-down horizon; **promotional/retention messaging** after completion, or *during a pending recovery window*, is treating the account as live. Where no wind-down is stated, a **7-day default horizon** applies. **Disclosed, legally required retention of specific records** (tax, AML/KYC, legal hold), stated at deletion, is not a violation — *presenting* such a deletion as total is. A terminal state the flow *presents* as deletion counts as deletion — a deactivation masquerading as deletion violates this check.
- **Modes:** behavioral
- **Evidence:** `screenshot` (terminal confirmation, stated terms), `flow_transcript` (post-deletion login attempt after the stated window; passive-interaction test), `notification_record` (post-deletion and in-window messaging), `contract_text`.
- **Procedure:** Complete the deletion (account, or covered data-deletion control); capture stated effective/recovery terms and any disclosed lawful-retention notice. During any recovery window: perform **no authenticated interaction** (the probe must not restart the clock — §9), and monitor the address for promotional messaging. After the stated window (where the observation window permits): attempt login / re-query the covered data and record silent restoration or persistence; monitor messaging against the stated (or default 7-day) wind-down horizon.
- **Decision (cascade — `fail` precedes `unobserved`, a permitted §4.6 deviation: the terms-based shapes are decidable from captured terms even when the post-window boundary is not):** `na` — no deletion path exists (check 4's finding; no completed deletion to assess). `fail` — the stated terms contradict deletion (the flow presents deletion but the terms describe deactivation or indefinite retention-for-reactivation without user choice — undisclosed-at-deletion lawful retention is *presented-as-total* and fails here too); **or** login after the stated recovery window silently restores the account/covered data; **or** the pending deletion was cancelled by a non-explicit interaction (passive-reset); **or** the account or covered data observably persists after completion; **or** promotional/retention messaging **recurs** during the recovery window or beyond the stated (or default 7-day) wind-down horizon. `unobserved` — no terms-decidable trap, but the post-window boundary **or the wind-down horizon** is not observable within the observation window → partial scope, CONDITIONAL — **not** `pass`. `conditional` — honored in all observable respects except a **single residual** promotional message past the horizon (a straggler, not a campaign — the tolerance edge). `pass` — terminal deletion confirmed; post-window boundary and wind-down horizon observed clean; any lawful retention disclosed at deletion.
- **Severity:** blocking
- *Evidence note (0.1.1, finding D-2):* third-party/enforcement evidence of dishonored deletion is **not a probe-decision input** — a probe cannot gather it, and stale findings would indict reformed behavior. It routes to the protocol's **challenge/revocation machinery** (design doc, Error Correction: anyone may submit evidence against a PASS). Corpus rows citing enforcement records remain valid adjudication substrate: raters decide them from the stated facts against this cascade, exactly as with the sibling's FTC anchors.

### Check 7 — `pre-deletion-export-prompt`
- **Statement (SHOULD):** Where an export path exists, the deletion flow SHOULD surface export availability (an affordance or clear pointer) before the terminal action — deleting is the moment data loss becomes irreversible, and a genuine exit offers the user their data on the way out.
- **Modes:** behavioral
- **Evidence:** `flow_transcript`, `screenshot` (pre-terminal screens).
- **Procedure:** Walk the deletion flow to (not through) the terminal action; record whether export is surfaced anywhere before it.
- **Decision (cascade):** `na` — no export path exists **at all** (a gated or paywalled path still counts as an export path for this check — surfacing it is still data-loss protection), or no deletion path exists (check 4's finding). `unobserved` — the deletion flow could not be reached within the window. `fail` — the flow reaches its terminal action with no export affordance or mention anywhere before it (→ contributes CONDITIONAL, not FAIL, since SHOULD). `pass` — export is surfaced before finalization. *(na scoping sharpened at 0.1.1 — finding D-23.)*
- **Severity:** caveat
- *Note:* a retention *offer* dressed as an export prompt still counts under check 5's rules; this check asks only whether export is genuinely surfaced.

## 5. Aggregation & verdicts (§4.4, §5)

Default aggregation, inherited from CRITERION-SPEC §4.4:

| Verdict | Condition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All blocking checks (1–6) `pass` (with `na` excluded and **at least one blocking check applying**), no check returns `conditional`, and check 7 is `pass`, `na`, or `unobserved`. |
| **CONDITIONAL** | No blocking `fail`, but ≥1 of: any check returns `conditional` (possible on 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 as currently decided); check 7 (`caveat`) `fail`s; a **blocking** check is `unobserved` (partial scope). MUST enumerate the triggering check_ids. |
| **FAIL** | Any blocking check (1–6) returns `fail`. Enumerate **all** failing blocking checks (CRITERION-SPEC §5.2). |
| **INDETERMINATE** | The probe could not provision an account or reach the exit surfaces at all; or model consensus not reached; **or every blocking check returned `na`** — no account and no data, the criterion did not apply (§3 Applicability). Treated as absent. |

Check 7 `unobserved` does not downgrade (non-blocking partial scope). Check 6 `unobserved` is expected to be **common** — recovery windows often exceed probe windows — and deliberately yields CONDITIONAL (partial scope) rather than PASS: an attestation that never observed the post-deletion boundary should say so. Operators SHOULD size observation windows past the stated recovery window where a clean PASS matters.

**Attribution.** On FAIL, enumerate all failing blocking checks; on CONDITIONAL, the triggering check(s) — machine-readable (CRITERION-SPEC §5.2).

**Confidence** (§5.3): `min` over contributing checks of `(model_agreement × evidence_completeness)`, where `evidence_completeness` reflects how much of each check's procedure completed (e.g., a check-6 decided with the post-window boundary observed scores higher than one decided from terms alone). Bands: high ≥ 0.90; medium ≥ 0.70 and < 0.90; low < 0.70 → downgrade to INDETERMINATE + route to review.
