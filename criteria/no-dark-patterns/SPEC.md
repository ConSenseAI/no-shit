## 1. Guarantee (Â§3.1)

The interfaces through which you choose â€” buying, consenting, declining â€” show you real information and honest controls. No fabricated countdowns, stock counts, or activity feeds; no shame-worded or trick-worded choices; no hidden or disguised options; declining is as easy as accepting; nothing is added to your order or account without your explicit choice; and a "no" is remembered, not worn down.

## 2. Harm model (Â§3.2)

Dark patterns extract at the **decision moment** â€” money, data, and attention are taken through choices the user did not mean to make â€” but the harm is a textbook credence good, and the deferral runs *deeper* here than for most extraction: the manipulated interface does not merely look normal at decision time, it looks **better** (an urgent deal, a popular product, a one-click consent). The harm arrives later and often invisibly:

- **On the bill** â€” an add-on that was never chosen, a charge from a control that didn't say it was a purchase (discovered at statement time, or never).
- **In the inbox and the data trail** â€” marketing enrollment and data consents extracted through pre-selection, asymmetric choices, or inverted wording (discovered as spam and as tracking, attributed to nothing).
- **Never** â€” the counterfactual choice is invisible to its own victim. A user pressured by a fabricated countdown into buying does not learn the timer was fake; a user who couldn't find the decline button does not know it existed. This is precisely why market feedback fails to price the behavior, and why the property must be verified rather than reviewed.

The burden falls hardest on the hurried, the elderly, the less digitally literate, and users operating in a second language â€” the populations with the least slack to parse a trick question or hunt for a suppressed decline control.

Grounding (Â§10): the FTC's dark-patterns framework (2022 staff report; Epic Games and Fashion Nova actions), the EU's interface-manipulation prohibitions (DSA Art. 25, UCPD guidance, CNIL consent-asymmetry decisions), CCPA's symmetry-in-choice rules, and the research taxonomies (Brignull; Mathur et al.; Gray et al.).

**Anti-creep anchor.** Every check below traces to one of three harms: *money taken through a choice the user didn't knowingly make*, *consent extracted the user didn't knowingly give*, or *a decision forced through fabricated decision-inputs*. A proposed check that traces to none of these does not belong in this criterion.

## 3. Scope (Â§3.3)

**Covers** â€” interface **choice architecture** on the product's covered surfaces:

- **Pressure signals** displayed where choices are made: urgency (countdowns, deadlines), scarcity (stock/demand claims), social proof (activity feeds, testimonials, review aggregates) â€” *their truthfulness, not their tone*.
- **Choice controls**: the wording, semantics, and visual presentation of accept/decline options; consent and permission prompts; defaults and pre-selections.
- **Order/account integrity**: what ends up in a cart, order, account, or enrollment versus what the user affirmatively chose.
- **Prompt recurrence**: whether a declined prompt stays declined.

**Covered surfaces** = purchase and checkout flows, consent and permission prompts, offer/upsell/enrollment prompts, **product-originated notifications** (email, push, in-app messages) that present offers or choices, and any surface where the product asks the user to choose â€” **except the surfaces excluded below.**

**Non-goals** (explicitly out of scope, owned elsewhere or deferred):

- **The cancellation flow and the auto-renewal disclosure/consent facts of signup** â†’ **`no-subscription-trap`**, which owns dark patterns on those surfaces (its Â§3.3; reciprocal exclusion here). The boundary is **fact-based, not flow-based**: on a signup screen, the auto-renewal disclosure and its consent control are theirs; *everything else* in the same flow â€” a fake countdown rendered beside that consent control (check 1), a pre-checked marketing box below it (check 6), a sneaked add-on (check 7) â€” is this criterion's. (Both specs state this identically as of `no-subscription-trap` 0.1.6.)
- **Price honesty and completeness** â€” drip pricing, hidden mandatory fees, misleading price framing â†’ **`transparent-pricing`**. - **The substance of data practices** â€” what is collected, shared, sold, retained â†’ **`no-surveillance`** (future criterion). This criterion owns *manipulation of the consent interface*; what happens to the data after consent is theirs.
- **Data export and account deletion** â†’ **`no-lock-in`** (including deletion-flow obstruction â€” the account-exit analogue of the cancellation carve-out).
- **Advertising and content claims** beyond the enumerated interface pressure signals â€” product-quality claims, endorsements, "pre-approved" offers â€” are deception law's domain, not interface choice architecture. A criterion that owned every false statement would own everything.

**Related criteria:**

## 4. Requirements â†’ Checks (Â§3.4, Â§4)

**On observation modes.** This criterion is deliberately **dual-mode**, and every check is `either`. The design doc stakes out `no-dark-patterns` as *"the criterion most judgeable from client-side source â€¦ the keystone test of code-audit judgment"* (MVP, Stage 0): fabricated urgency is a `Date.now()+600` seed with no backend deadline; fabricated social proof is a hardcoded name array on an RNG interval; an inverted checkbox is a negated handler; a suppressed decline control is a CSS rule. Per CRITERION-SPEC Â§4.2, the attestation records per check which mode actually adjudicated it. Where the deciding logic is server-side and only behavioral evidence can reach it, a code-mode run returns `unobserved` for that check rather than guessing. (This is the first multi-mode criterion authored against the mold â€” a deliberate test that per-check modes and `either` hold up, the complement of `no-subscription-trap`'s single-mode test.)

**Mode-join rule (when both modes run in one audit).** Per-check outcomes from different modes join by a **total lattice, not a vote**:

- Among **decided** outcomes, the join is worst-of: `fail` > `conditional` > `pass`. Evidence union, never exoneration: a clean codebase does not exonerate an observed live violation (server-injected is still what shipped), and an innocent-looking rendered capture does not exonerate coded fabrication (the RNG *is* the fabrication).
- A check **decided in any mode is decided**. `unobserved` in the other mode reflects that *mode's* reach, not the check's observability â€” it never downgrades a decided outcome. `unobserved` survives the join only when **every** mode that ran returned it. (Without this rule, a both-modes audit â€” the design doc's highest assurance tier â€” could yield a strictly weaker verdict than a behavioral-only one.)
- `na` joins only with `na`: if one mode decides the check while the other returns `na`, the decided outcome governs â€” one mode found the surface the other missed.

(This is CRITERION-SPEC Â§4.7's default lattice, promoted to the mold at 0.6.0 from this criterion's finding T-11 â€” Â§11.)

### Check 1 â€” `no-false-urgency-or-scarcity`
- **Statement (MUST NOT):** Covered surfaces MUST NOT display urgency or scarcity signals that are false: countdown timers or deadlines not tied to a real expiry (they reset, persist past zero, or gate nothing that actually changes); "limited time" claims with no real limit; stock, availability, or demand claims ("only 3 left", "12 people viewing") not backed by the real state they purport to report; or a technically-real signal deployed so that the urgency it communicates about the **material** decision is false (see decision).
- **Modes:** either
- **Evidence:** `source_ref` (timer seeding, stock/viewer-count generation), `dom_snapshot`, `screenshot`, `flow_transcript` (cross-session/cross-identity comparison, post-expiry re-visit), `har_capture` (whether displayed counts come from a backend at all), `notification_record` (urgency signals in product-originated emails/push).
- **Procedure:** Inventory urgency/scarcity signals on covered surfaces, including captured product-originated notifications. CODE: trace each displayed value to its source â€” server-supplied state vs client-side generation (RNG, constants, per-load seeds). BEHAVIORAL: revisit across sessions/identities and past the stated expiry; record whether the timer resets, the offer survives its own deadline, or the counts regenerate implausibly.
- **Decision (cascade, first match wins â€” CRITERION-SPEC Â§4.6):** `na` â€” no urgency or scarcity signals on any covered surface. `unobserved` â€” signals present, but the falsification procedure could not complete within the window (e.g., no post-expiry or cross-session observation possible, and no code access). `fail` â€” any displayed signal is fabricated: the timer resets or its expiry changes nothing (the offer persists); the claimed limit does not exist; a count is generated client-side or by rule with no backing state; or a real signal about an immaterial element is presented so as to fabricate urgency about the material one (a real "free sticker" countdown juxtaposed to imply the cart price expires â€” the price urgency communicated is false). `conditional` â€” every signal is literally true but engineered to mislead at the margin: a real, expiring timer whose equivalent offer relaunches promptly or on a recurring cadence â€” an overnight gap does not break seriality (serial "flash" sales making permanent pricing look time-limited; wording sharpened at 0.1.3, P-2). `pass` â€” otherwise: every displayed signal is backed by real state (or there are honest signals only).
- **Severity:** blocking
- *Boundary:* the tone or prominence of a **true** signal is not this check's concern â€” truthful urgency is legitimate commerce. Falsity is the predicate.

### Check 2 â€” `no-fabricated-social-proof`
- **Statement (MUST NOT):** Covered surfaces MUST NOT display social-proof signals that are fabricated or that misrepresent the data behind them: activity notifications ("X just bought Y") generated rather than drawn from real events; testimonials or reviews that do not exist or were not made by users; or displayed aggregates (ratings, counts) curated to misrepresent the underlying data â€” including suppressing negative entries while presenting the remainder as the whole.
- **Modes:** either
- **Evidence:** `source_ref` (static name/message arrays, notification generators, review-filter logic), `har_capture` (whether activity data is fetched from any backend), `dom_snapshot`, `flow_transcript` (cross-session/cross-identity comparison), `screenshot`, `notification_record` (social-proof claims in product-originated messages).
- **Procedure:** Inventory social-proof elements, including those in captured product-originated notifications. CODE: trace displayed events/aggregates to their source; flag client-side generation and filtered aggregation, and operator-side configuration of third-party display thresholds. BEHAVIORAL: compare feeds across fresh identities and sessions (identical "recent" sequences to every visitor indicate generation); cross-check displayed aggregates against enumerable detail (review counts vs listed reviews).
- **Decision (cascade â€” `fail` precedes `unobserved`, a permitted Â§4.6 deviation: suppression/mismatch shapes are decidable from captured evidence even when full aggregate verification is not):** `na` â€” no social-proof elements on covered surfaces. `fail` â€” a signal is fabricated (generated events, invented testimonials) or the displayed aggregate misrepresents the underlying data (negative entries suppressed from an aggregate presented as complete). `unobserved` â€” elements present but not falsifiable within the window: neither source tracing nor cross-session comparison was possible; **or** the aggregate's composition is **operator-controlled** (a configurable third-party widget with display thresholds/filters) and its completeness cannot be verified in any mode run â€” unverifiable *curated* aggregates are partial scope, never a silent pass. (A plain third-party embed whose composition the operator does not control is not, by itself, unverifiable.) `conditional` â€” signals are drawn from real data but framed to mislead at the margin: real events presented with false recency ("just bought" toasts replaying days-old orders as live). `pass` â€” otherwise: displayed social proof traces to real events/users and aggregates reflect the underlying data.
- **Severity:** blocking

### Check 3 â€” `no-confirmshaming`
- **Statement (MUST NOT):** On covered surfaces, declining an offer, enrollment, or consent MUST NOT require selecting a control **worded or depicted** to shame, belittle, or guilt the decliner â€” whether by self-disparagement ("No thanks, I like paying full price"), by editorializing the decline as harmful or foolish ("No, I don't want to protect my family"), or by shame-laden imagery attached to the decline path (a weeping mascot on the "no" control).
- **Modes:** either
- **Evidence:** `screenshot`, `dom_snapshot` (the decline control's text and attached imagery), `source_ref` (string resources/templates/assets), `notification_record` (decline controls inside product-originated messages).
- **Procedure:** Inventory offer/consent prompts on covered surfaces, including captured product-originated notifications; capture the exact wording **and visual depiction** of each decline control and its immediate framing.
- **Decision (cascade):** `na` â€” no prompts with an accept/decline choice on covered surfaces. `unobserved` â€” such prompts exist but their decline controls could not be captured within the window. `fail` â€” any decline control is shame-worded or shame-depicted (self-disparaging; editorializing the decline as harmful/foolish/shameful; or shame-laden imagery attached to the decline path). `conditional` â€” decline controls are neutrally worded and depicted, but the prompt's surrounding copy or imagery leans on **emotive or judgment-laden loss framing beyond factual consequence** to pressure the choice, while both options remain neutrally labeled. `pass` â€” otherwise: declining is available through neutrally worded, neutrally depicted controls, and any consequence statements are factual.
- **Severity:** blocking
- *Boundary:* stating a **factual, specific consequence** of declining ("canceling backup means files older than 30 days won't be recoverable") is not confirmshaming and does not trigger the conditional band â€” the band requires emotive or judgment-laden framing *beyond* factual consequence ("don't leave your family unprotected"). Weaponizing the choice control itself â€” words or imagery â€” is the fail shape. (`clean-syn-factual-consequence` and `bound-syn-guilt-copy` anchor the two sides.)

### Check 4 â€” `no-trick-wording`
- **Statement (MUST NOT):** Choice controls on covered surfaces MUST NOT carry wording or semantics that invert or materially misstate their effect: double negatives ("Uncheck to not receiveâ€¦"); checkboxes/toggles whose label polarity is reversed relative to their effect; mixed opt-in/opt-out semantics within one form engineered to confuse; **inconsistent mappings of the same control to different effects across adjacent contexts** (a button that previews on one screen and purchases on the next); or action controls whose label materially misstates what they do â€” including controls that place a charge or commitment without saying so.
- **Modes:** either
- **Evidence:** `dom_snapshot` (labels vs control state), `source_ref` (handlers â€” what the control actually toggles/submits), `screenshot`, `flow_transcript` (what following the control actually did).
- **Procedure:** Inventory choice controls on covered surfaces; for each, compare the label's plain-language meaning against the control's actual effect (CODE: the handler; BEHAVIORAL: exercise it and observe). Flag negation stacking, reversed polarity, mixed semantics, and labelâ†”effect mismatches.
- **Decision (cascade):** `na` â€” no choice controls on covered surfaces (no forms, toggles, or action prompts). `unobserved` â€” controls exist but their effects could not be established within the window (not exercisable, no code access). `fail` â€” any control's wording or semantics invert or materially misstate its effect, **including**: a double negative or reversed polarity governing any covered choice control; adjacent controls mixing opt-in and opt-out semantics such that a consistent reading produces the wrong result; the same control mapped to materially different effects in adjacent contexts; or an action control effecting a charge/commitment its label does not state. (The instance list is non-exhaustive; the umbrella predicate governs.) `pass` â€” otherwise: control labels state their effects plainly and polarity and mappings are consistent.
- **Severity:** blocking
- *Boundary:* verbose-but-accurate wording is not trick wording (density without inversion passes); inversion, reversal, and misstatement are the predicates. Whether a charge-placing control was *presented as a choice at all* is check 7's question; *mislabeled* controls are this check's. And check 4 owns what the control **says** â€” rendered selection/progress state contradicting reality (a highlight on the unchosen plan) is check 5's misrepresented-state shape, not this check's umbrella (dry-run finding P-1: the umbrella clause invited double-ownership; one owner per fact).

### Check 5 â€” `no-visual-interference`
- **Statement (MUST NOT):** On covered surfaces, the product MUST NOT use visual presentation to hide or disguise choices: a decline/close/opt-out option rendered effectively undiscoverable (contrast, size, or placement far outside where a user would reasonably look â€” concealment); interactive elements disguised as something else (ads styled as content or system UI, fake close buttons, simulated OS dialogs); or visual state that misrepresents what is selected.
- **Modes:** either
- **Evidence:** `screenshot` (rendered presentation â€” primary), `dom_snapshot` (computed styles, z-order, positioning), `source_ref` (styling/overlay logic).
- **Procedure:** For each covered surface with competing options, capture the rendered presentation; record the discoverability of the disfavored option (contrast, size, position, occlusion) and whether any interactive element masquerades as content or system UI. CODE corroborates with the styling rules that produce the rendering.
- **Decision (cascade):** `na` â€” no covered surfaces presenting competing options or third-party/promotional elements. `unobserved` â€” such surfaces exist but rendered presentation could not be captured within the window. `fail` â€” an available option is concealed (rendered at near-invisible contrast/size, positioned where a user would not reasonably find it, or occluded); an interactive element is disguised as content or system UI; **or displayed selection/progress state contradicts the actual state** (a toggle rendered on while off; a plan highlighted as selected that was not chosen). `conditional` â€” the disfavored option is legible and discoverable in-flow but **materially subordinated** (e.g., a small plain text-link set against a large accent button) â€” visible, but styled to be overlooked. `pass` â€” otherwise: competing options are presented with ordinary emphasis differences at most (both legible, discoverable, presented as controls) and rendered state matches actual state.
- **Severity:** blocking
- *Boundary:* three calibrated bands â€” ordinary CTA emphasis (both options clearly presented) = `pass`; legible-but-materially-subordinated = `conditional`; effectively undiscoverable or disguised = `fail`. Within this criterion's surfaces only; the cancel-control analogue inside cancellation flows is `no-subscription-trap` check 3's.

### Check 6 â€” `choice-symmetry`
- **Statement (MUST):** Where a covered surface asks the user to accept or decline something **optional** â€” a data/marketing consent, a permission, an optional add-on or enrollment; *optional is judged normatively*: a choice that could lawfully or functionally be declined while still obtaining the underlying product, regardless of how the product presents it â€” declining MUST be available with parity: reachable in **no more user actions than accepting**, present on the **same surface** where acceptance is offered, and never **pre-selected** toward acceptance (no pre-ticked consent boxes, no default-on optional add-ons presented as chosen). Parity extends over the choice's **lifecycle**: a decline MUST be **persisted no less durably** than an acceptance, and **withdrawing** a previously granted consent MUST NOT require more actions than granting it did.
- **Modes:** either
- **Evidence:** `flow_transcript`, `step_count` (actions to accept vs actions to decline), `dom_snapshot` (default states, what the first layer presents), `screenshot`, `source_ref` (default values, branch structure).
- **Procedure:** Inventory optional accept/decline prompts on covered surfaces (optional judged normatively â€” see statement). For each: count minimum user actions to complete acceptance and to complete declination; record whether decline is present on the first surface or requires navigation; record default states of consent/add-on controls. Record how each choice is **persisted** (CODE: cookie/flag TTLs and re-trigger conditions for accepted vs declined states). Where a consent was granted, exercise **withdrawal** and count its actions against the grant's.
- **Decision (cascade):** `na` â€” no normatively optional accept/decline prompts on covered surfaces. `unobserved` â€” prompts exist but the decline path could not be exercised or counted within the window (e.g., variant-gated). `fail` â€” declining requires **more actions** than accepting (including exactly one more â€” reject behind an expander or second layer when accept is one action); or decline is absent from the surface where acceptance is offered (reachable only via navigation elsewhere â€” a consent wall for a normatively optional purpose is this shape); or the optional choice is pre-selected toward acceptance; or the **declined state is persisted less durably** than the accepted state (decline honored but re-asked while acceptances persist â€” TTL/flag asymmetry); or **withdrawal** of a granted consent requires more actions than the grant did. `pass` â€” otherwise: decline available in actions â‰¤ accept, on the same surface, defaults neutral or off, declines persisted with parity, withdrawal no harder than grant.
- **Severity:** blocking
- *Boundary:* deliberately strict, with no `conditional` band: action-count parity is the crispest operationalization of "declining as easy as accepting" (CCPA Â§7004's symmetry rule; the CNIL consent decisions; EDPB taskforce), and a strict opening position is free to loosen while `draft` (T-4 window) if calibration shows humans read one-expand-reject as a caveat rather than a violation â€” flagged in Â§12. The lifecycle prongs are the same rule extended in time: persistence parity is the structural, code-decidable answer to "reject that quietly un-rejects" (and pairs with check 8, which owns re-prompt *frequency*); withdrawal parity is GDPR Art. 7(3)'s "as easy to withdraw as to give" made countable â€” consent *withdrawal* is owned here, while subscription *cancellation* remains `no-subscription-trap`'s and account *deletion* `no-lock-in`'s. This check owns the **structural** facts (counts, presence, defaults, persistence); the *visual styling* of the same prompt is check 5's, and its *wording* is checks 3/4's â€” one owner per fact, no double-counting. The auto-renewal consent control at signup is `no-subscription-trap` check 5's; a marketing checkbox on the same screen is this check's.

### Check 7 â€” `no-unconsented-additions`
- **Statement (MUST NOT):** The product MUST NOT add items, services, enrollments, or charges to a user's order or account without an affirmative choice: no sneaking items into a cart; no bundling an unrelated enrollment silently into another action; no charging an interaction that was not presented as a purchase or commitment; and no charge-committing control **engineered for inadvertent activation** (placed or timed so that stray input charges â€” adjacency to unrelated controls, layout shift under the pointer, single-press charging from a browsing or loading context â€” with no confirmation step).
- **Modes:** either
- **Evidence:** `flow_transcript` (what was selected vs what the order/account contains), `screenshot` (order review, receipts), `dom_snapshot`, `notification_record` (order confirmations), `source_ref` (cart-mutation/enrollment logic), `har_capture`.
- **Procedure:** Exercise the purchase/enrollment flow selecting only explicit choices; diff the resulting order/account state against the user's affirmative selections. CODE: inspect cart-mutation and enrollment triggers for paths not gated on an explicit user choice.
- **Decision (cascade):** `na` â€” no order, enrollment, or charge-capable flows on covered surfaces. `unobserved` â€” flows exist but order/account state could not be reached or read within the window (e.g., payment stage not exercisable). `fail` â€” anything appears in the order or account that the user never affirmatively selected: a silently added line item; an enrollment bundled unstated into an unrelated action; a charge triggered by an interaction not presented as a purchase/commitment; or a charge-committing control engineered for inadvertent activation (adjacency/layout-shift/single-press-from-browsing, no confirmation step) â€” whether or not a mis-hit was observed in the window. `conditional` â€” the addition was disclosed on the committing surface but not separately choosable (a stated extra bundled into a single "Continue" with no independent opt-out control) â€” disclosed, but consent was not distinct. `pass` â€” otherwise: order and account contain exactly what was affirmatively chosen; optional extras were offered unselected.
- **Severity:** blocking
- *Boundary:* the **amount or honesty of a disclosed price** is `transparent-pricing`'s; this check owns whether the *addition was chosen*. A pre-selected add-on **presented as a choice** is check 6's pre-selection shape; an addition **never presented as a choice at all** is this check's. Recurring-billing consent is `no-subscription-trap` check 5's.

### Check 8 â€” `no-nagging-after-decline`
- **Statement (SHOULD NOT):** A prompt the user has declined (a rating request, notification/permission ask, upsell, install banner) SHOULD NOT be re-presented within the same session, re-presented so often across sessions that it functions as wearing the user down, or re-presented so as to block task progress.
- **Modes:** either
- **Evidence:** `flow_transcript` (decline events and re-presentations, with timestamps), `screenshot`, `source_ref` (re-prompt scheduling/persistence logic).
- **Procedure:** Decline each recurring-prompt type once; continue using the product across the observation window; record every re-presentation of a declined prompt (same-session, cross-session counts, task-blocking behavior). CODE corroborates via the persistence and re-trigger logic.
- **Decision (cascade):** `na` â€” no declinable recurring prompts on covered surfaces, or none were declined during observation. `unobserved` â€” prompts were declined but the window was too short to observe recurrence behavior. `fail` â€” a declined prompt re-presents within the same session; or a declined prompt type re-presents **three or more** times across the observation window; or total re-presentations of declined prompts **across all types** reach **four or more** in the window (the type-carousel shape â€” no single type crosses its own line while the product nags in aggregate); or a re-presentation blocks task progress until re-answered. `conditional` â€” a declined prompt type re-presents exactly **twice** across the window, and the cross-type total stays under four (persistent, at the tolerance edge, but not wearing-down). `pass` â€” otherwise: declined prompts stay dismissed for the session and reappear at most once each, fewer than four total, across the window.
- **Severity:** caveat
- *Note:* thresholds (same-session; per-type 2 vs â‰Ą3; cross-type â‰Ą4) are deliberately explicit so two adjudicators converge, and are first-draft values for calibration to stress (Â§12).
